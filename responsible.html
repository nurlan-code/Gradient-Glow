Understanding Problem Gambling

While Gradient Glow doesn't involve real money, we believe it's important to educate users about the serious issue of problem gambling.

What is Problem Gambling?

Problem gambling (also called gambling disorder or gambling addiction) is the urge to gamble continuously despite harmful negative consequences or a desire to stop. It affects millions of people worldwide and can lead to:

  • Severe financial problems and debt
  • Relationship difficulties and family breakdowns
  • Mental health issues including depression and anxiety
  • Job loss and career problems
  • Legal issues
  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
Important: If you find yourself thinking about real money gambling after using our demo platform, please be aware of the risks and consider seeking help from the resources listed below.

Tips for Healthy Gaming Habits

Whether using demo platforms like ours or considering real gambling, here are important guidelines:

For Demo Gaming (like Gradient Glow):

  • Remember that these are simulations - real gambling has serious financial risks
  • Use demo platforms for learning and entertainment only
  • Don't let demo gaming lead you to real money gambling sites
  • Set time limits even for free gaming to maintain life balance

If You Choose to Gamble with Real Money:

  • Never gamble with money you can't afford to lose
  • Set strict budget limits before you start
  • Never chase losses - accept them and move on
  • Don't gamble when emotional, stressed, or under the influence
  • Take regular breaks and set time limits
  • Never borrow money to gamble
  • Keep gambling as entertainment, not a way to make money
  • Use self-exclusion tools if you feel you're losing control
